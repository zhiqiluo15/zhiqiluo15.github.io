鸦片战争

The Opium Wars

晚清 · 工业革命 ／ 1839–1860

英商以鸦片扭转贸易逆差，林则徐虎门销烟，

坚船利炮叩开广州、厦门、定海、天津，

《南京条约》割香港、开五口，

炮舰外交改写东亚贸易版图。

白银外流、关税尽失，帝国裂痕自此不可弥合。

史

In the early nineteenth century, British merchants flooded China with opium to reverse a trade deficit. When Commissioner Lin Zexu confiscated and destroyed the drug at Humen, Britain responded with gunboat diplomacy.

The Treaty of Nanjing (1842) forced the Qing empire to cede Hong Kong Island, open five treaty ports, and pay massive indemnities. A second war (1856–1860) brought further concessions — the burning of the Old Summer Palace and the legalization of the opium trade.

These conflicts marked the violent collision of an industrializing West with a declining agrarian empire, and the vocabulary born from this era — gunboat, treaty, cede, opium — still echoes in international relations today.

拆

鸦片 opium /ˈəʊpiəm/

a reddish-brown addictive drug prepared from the juice of the opium poppy, historically traded as a commodity

"British merchants flooded China with opium" — 鸦片贸易扭转逆差

炮舰 gunboat /ˈɡʌnbəʊt/

a small warship armed with heavy guns, used for coastal bombardment and intimidation

"Britain responded with gunboat diplomacy" — 坚船利炮叩开国门

条约 treaty /ˈtriːti/

a formally concluded and ratified agreement between states

"The Treaty of Nanjing forced the Qing empire..." — 《南京条约》

割让 cede /siːd/

to give up power or territory, especially by treaty

"...to cede Hong Kong Island" — 割让香港岛

帝国 empire /ˈempaɪər/

an extensive group of states ruled over by a single monarch or sovereign authority

"The Qing empire" — 碰撞中的大清帝国

口岸 port /pɔːrt/

a harbor where ships load and unload cargo; a treaty port is one opened to foreign trade by treaty

"Five treaty ports" — 五口通商：广州、厦门、福州、宁波、上海

赔款 indemnity /ɪnˈdemnɪti/

a sum of money paid as compensation, especially by a defeated nation to the victor

"...and pay massive indemnities" — 巨额战争赔款

特许 concession /kənˈseʃn/

a grant of land or property by a government in return for services, often under foreign control

"...further concessions" — 租界与治外法权
