茶

Tea · Cha · Chai

一条叶子，两种读音的世界旅行

茶叶从福建泉州港装船出海，

闽南语的「te」随荷兰商船漂洋过海，

化为英语 tea、法语 thé、德语 Tee。

另一路沿陆上丝绸之路西行，

北方官话的「cha」经波斯、印度，

落地为印地语 chai、阿拉伯语 shay、俄语 chay。

一片叶子，海陆分途——

今天世界语言中「茶」的读音只有两种，

都来自中国。

源

The word tea entered English in the 1650s via the Dutch thee, borrowed from the Min Nan Chinese tê — the language spoken in the port of Xiamen (Amoy), where Dutch traders first loaded tea onto ships bound for Europe.

Meanwhile, the Mandarin pronunciation cha traveled overland along the Silk Road through Persia and into the Indian subcontinent, giving rise to chai in Hindi, shay in Arabic, and chay in Russian.

Linguists can map historical trade routes by whether a language uses a "tea-word" (sea route) or a "cha-word" (land route). Portuguese borrowed chá from Cantonese via Macau; Japan uses cha. Nearly every language on Earth falls into one of these two etymological families — both rooted in Chinese.

拆

茶（海路） tea /tiː/

a hot drink made by infusing dried crushed leaves in boiling water; the word entered English via Dutch thee from Min Nan tê

From Min Nan tê → Dutch thee → English tea

茶（陆路） cha /tʃɑː/

the Mandarin and Cantonese pronunciation of 茶, adopted by languages along the Silk Road; the source of Japanese cha, Hindi chai, Russian chay

"Languages using a cha-word" — 陆上丝绸之路的茶之路

香料茶 chai /tʃaɪ/

in English, a type of Indian tea made by boiling tea leaves with milk, sugar, and spices; from Hindi chai (चाय), ultimately from Chinese cha

"Masala chai" — 从印度回到英语的「回旋镖」词汇

瓷器 porcelain /ˈpɔːrsəlɪn/

a white vitrified translucent ceramic; named via Italian porcellana (cowrie shell), from its resemblance to the glossy surface

Teacups of porcelain — 茶与瓷，海路的双生子

商队 caravan /ˈkærəvæn/

a group of travelers journeying together through desert or hostile regions; from Persian kārvān, the vehicle of Silk Road trade

"Caravans carried cha along the Silk Road" — 驼铃声中的茶叶

丝绸 silk /sɪlk/

a fine lustrous fiber produced by silkworms; from Old English seolc, ultimately from Greek sērikos ("of the Seres" — the Chinese)

"The Silk Road" — 希腊人用「丝国人」命名中国
